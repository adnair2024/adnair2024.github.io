    Stationary Wrap Up - 2025

    December 22, 2025

    Physical Stationary That Brings Me Daily Enjoyment

    In a world where we use technology all day, I think using physical writing tools is really beneficial. I wake up every morning and write in my journal, about the day before. This helps me keep filter the import parts of the previous day in my mind, and also is a nice part of my daily routine that helps me say active. In this article, I’ll show my the physical tools I use in my day to day life. Below, I’ll provide Amazon links to the products I’ve purchased.

    Main Journaling Pen - Aihao X97 0.5mm

    I love this pen. I purchased a 10 pack of these pens around a month ago, and for journaling and multipurpose note-taking, and I’ve enjoyed this ever since. For someone who journals every day, and plans projects on physical paper, it’s a real pleasure to write on. I chose to get black ink, even though you can get a mixed pack or red only or blue only. The transparent color of the pen makes it look nice when resting on your notebook. The 0.5mm nib makes it easy to write small, which is important for me when I am scribbling notes every day. It’s a great and affordable daily driver. Ink doesn’t skip at all, and if it does this is a refillable pen so you can just swap the refill. The ink unfortunately does take a minute or so to dry, which can create some smudging but nothing major. The ink doesn’t bleed at all onto the back of the page, making it great for using both sides of the paper.

    Annotation Pen - Generic Roller Ball Pen 0.7mm (Blue)

    Unfortunately, I can’t find the actual name of this pen. It’s an amazing pen, and if I liked using blue ink for my notes I would probably use it. It’s a 0.7 mm nib size, so it’s a little thicker than the Aihao pen I use for planning and journaling. This makes it great for editing notes that I wrote previously. The ink dries faster than the Aihao. The only drawback of this pen is really that it feels cheap in your hand; chunky but light. Well, that and the slight bleeding onto the other side of the pen. This pen feels like if it drops, it would shatter into 30 pieces. The amazing writing quality makes up for the cheap build quality.

    Journal - Mnemosyne A5 Notebook

    This is my stationary pride and joy. This is the best notebook I’ve ever used. Mnemosyne is a company I’ve been using on and off since 2020, and they have never let me down. The notebook is built extremely well, with double spiral binding and possibly some of the best quality paper I’ve ever seen. The paper is glossy, but not cheap or too slick. It retains ink really well and I couldn’t suggest a better notebook. In addition to the quality of this product, the size makes it perfect. This is an A5 notebook, and is perfect for a smaller notebook or bag, and can accompany you everywhere. The pages are perforated, so you can tear out pages with ease. This makes it significantly better for notes or todo lists that you can quickly get ripped off.

    Miscellaneous Notebook - Pen+Gear Simulated Leather Notebook

    This is a cheap simulated leather notebook that I purchased at Walmart, and I’m pretty surprised about how much use I’ve gotten out of it. I used the brown variant that came in the two pack and basically finished it, and now I’ve moved on to the yellow (or “golden pond”) color and it has not disappointed yet. The simulated leather allows for the notebook to be really slim and cheap. Good crisp paper is nice, an opposite of the glossy and sleek paper to the paper in the Mnemosyne notebook’s paper. A notebook you’ll enjoy using and won’t lose sleep over losing.

    Pencil Case

    I bought this pencil case on a whim, and it’s paid off. I’m very happy with it, the quality is really amazing for $20, and it holds pens, pencils, pencil lead, and lip balm. To easily access this, you can pull out the drawer style pen holder. What stands out is the build quality; not sure if this is real leather or faux, but it and the inner suede is stunning. A pleasure to use.

    Conclusion

    While my digital tools definitely do the most in my workflow, these physical items are what makes my personal routine feel intentional. I like the mental clarity that comes from the friction of pen on paper, that digital writing cannot give us. Whether it’s the Aihao X97 0.5mm pen, or the Mnemosyne notebook, having these makes me want to write and stick to my routine. I think these are a great way to combat screen fatigue and keep productive.
